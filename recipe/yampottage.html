Yam Porridge

Description

Asaro which is also known as Yam pottage or Yam porridge is a very delicious Yam recipe though it’s eaten by most tribes in Nigeria however, it is more common in the Yoruba speaking parts of Nigeria. It’s also easy to make. If you love yam, it’s a

Ingredients

  • 1/2 Yam medium-sized Puna
  • 1 Onion medium-sized (diced)
  • 1.5 Pepper Sauce a blend of peppers, tomatoes, and Onions
  • 1/2 Cup Palm Oil of fresh
  • 2 Tablespoons Crayfish
  • Crab Meat Stockfish or dried fish
  • Parsley or any Leafy vegetables (spinach, kale, ugwu)

    • Pepper Sauce

  • 1 Red Bell Pepper
  • 2 Tomatoes
  • 1 Habanero Pepper
  • 1 Onion Small

Instructions

  1. Peel and cut the Yam into medium-sized chunks and rinse a couple of times
  2. Put the Yams inside a Pot, add water just to the level of the Yams and let it cook for 15 minutes on medium to high heat
  3. Add the pepper sauce palm oil, onions, crayfish, salt, and stock cubes, and allow it to cook for an additional 10 minutes on low heat
  4. Add the crabmeat (You can use whatever addition you prefer at this point like, Smoked Fish, Prawns, or smoked Turkey
  5. Mash the Yams with a Potato masher or a wooden spoon. I love to mash some yams and keep some chunks. Gives more texture!
  6. Stir in the parsley
  7. Allow it to simmer for an additional 3 to 5 minutes and enjoy while still hot!
